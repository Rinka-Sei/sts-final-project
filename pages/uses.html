The Uses of Artificial Intelligence

How AI is used worldwide and taking into account the following factors

Culture

When conducting online searches, biases in AI algorithms often become apparent. For instance, a search for "greatest leaders of all time" predominantly displays male figures, reflecting societal stereotypes. UNESCO emphasizes the importance of avoiding gender bias in AI to ensure fair and accurate representation, as outlined in the UNESCO Recommendation on the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence.

Economy

The increasing integration of AI into political campaigns, as demonstrated by the Republican National Committee's groundbreaking AI-generated ad during the 2023 elections, has raised significant concerns.

Experts emphasize various issues, including the potential for misinformation fueled by social media influence and declining trust in traditional media. Additionally, the rising sophistication of deep fakes poses a considerable threat to the integrity of political discourse.

Recent incidents, such as a deep fake video falsely depicting President Biden, underscore the ease with which AI-generated content can propagate false information. This highlights the need for a vigilant approach to combat the potential misuse of AI in shaping public opinion during election cycles.

Beyond geopolitical threats, vulnerable populations, especially women of color candidates, face disproportionate targeting in online mis- and disinformation campaigns. This underscores the broader societal impact and ethical considerations surrounding the use of AI in political communication.

The concept of the "liars dividend" adds another layer of concern, suggesting that realistic deep fakes could provide politicians with a convenient excuse to evade accountability. This potential consequence further contributes to the erosion of public trust in political messaging and statements.

Social media platforms, including Meta and TikTok, are grappling with the challenge of regulating AI-generated content. This has prompted legislative responses, such as Representative Yvette Clark's "REAL Political Advertisements Act," aimed at ensuring transparency in the use of AI in political advertising.

As discussions on AI regulation gain momentum, there is a growing recognition of the urgent need to address potential threats posed by AI in the political realm. This awareness becomes particularly crucial with another election cycle underway, emphasizing the importance of responsible and ethical deployment of AI technologies in the political landscape.
